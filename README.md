## Engineering intelligence starts here.

Every outage begins as a signal buried in noise. Traditional monitoring tells you what *happened*. OrbitForesight tells you what *will* happen, how much it *costs*, and what to *do* about it — before production breaks.

This is not a dashboard. It is a **decision intelligence platform** that transforms engineering telemetry into executive action. Six interconnected intelligence engines working in sequence: detect, investigate, correlate, analyze, report, execute.

---

## The $9,000-per-minute blind spot

Engineering organizations operate with a critical information asymmetry. Deployment velocity increases year over year, but incident response processes remain manual, reactive, and disconnected from business outcomes.

The cost of this asymmetry compounds across every layer:

| Layer | The Problem | The Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Detection** | Alerts fire after customers report issues | $9,000/min average enterprise downtime |
| **Investigation** | Engineers spend 6–8 hours per incident hunting root cause | $288K annual engineering waste per 10-person team |
| **Dependency Analysis** | No live blast radius mapping | 70% of P0 incidents cascade from 3+ undetected signals |
| **Business Impact** | Metrics without context | SLA credits, customer churn, revenue unrecoverable |
| **Reporting** | Manual postmortems with no decision intelligence | Recurring incidents with no systemic fix |

Reactive monitoring was designed for a world where engineers could keep up. That world no longer exists.

## The executive investigation workflow

OrbitForesight guides engineering leadership through a complete decision journey — from anomaly detection to remediation execution. Each step builds on the previous, creating a narrative arc from raw signal to strategic action.

### 1. Situation Awareness
*What is happening right now?*

The platform surfaces 3 critical indicators: anomaly count, AI confidence score, and revenue exposure. Engineering leaders see the complete risk posture of their infrastructure in under five seconds — no dashboards to navigate, no queries to write.

### 2. Root Cause Investigation
*Why did it happen?*

Fifteen forensic analysis dimensions execute in parallel — deployment correlation, dependency traversal, code change analysis, configuration drift detection, traffic pattern deviation. The AI ranks potential root causes by confidence score and presents the most likely candidate with supporting evidence.

### 3. Historical Pattern Match
*When has this happened before?*

The incident time machine reconstructs every prior occurrence of the same failure pattern. Timeline reconstruction with service-level granularity, root cause correlation across 1,598+ historical events, and AI-generated prevention recommendations with weighted confidence.

### 4. Dependency Impact Mapping
*What else will break?*

The live knowledge graph visualizes cascading failure propagation across every service, database, API gateway, and infrastructure dependency. Color-coded severity, weighted edge analysis, and blast radius calculation — before any remediation action is taken.

### 5. Business Impact Report
*What is the cost of inaction?*

Quantified revenue exposure, customer impact analysis, SLA risk calculation, and compliance implications — synthesized into a boardroom-ready intelligence report with ROI projections and prioritized strategic recommendations.

### 6. Execution Planning
*What should we do next?*

AI-generated remediation plans with effort estimation, resource allocation, team assignment, sprint breakdown, and risk mitigation. Every plan is derived from dependency intelligence and historical velocity data — not gut feel.

## Core innovations

### Predictive Engineering Intelligence

The risk engine processes 2.4M+ data points per analysis cycle, correlating deployment history, incident patterns, dependency topology, and team velocity into a unified risk score. ML-powered prediction achieves 94% accuracy across all signal types — detecting failure patterns that threshold-based monitoring misses entirely.

### Incident Replay Engine

Full forensic timeline reconstruction for every incident. Engineers replay any past failure with service-level granularity, root cause identification, and automated prevention recommendation generation. The replay engine cross-correlates against 1,598+ historical events to surface recurrence patterns and confidence-weighted root cause candidates.

### Dependency Intelligence Graph

847+ service nodes, 1,200+ dependency edges, live topology with automatic discovery. Each node carries risk weight, incident density, deployment velocity, and team ownership metadata. Edge weights encode traffic volume, criticality, and historical correlation strength.

### Blast Radius Mapping

Real-time failure propagation simulation across the entire service topology. Before any change, engineers visualize which services degrade, which customers are affected, and what revenue is at risk — with configurable failure scenarios and cascading impact modeling.

### Executive AI Reporting

Every metric is contextualized with business impact. The platform answers four questions automatically: What is happening? Why is it happening? What will happen next? What should leadership do? Reports translate engineering data into executive decisions with quantified ROI, confidence scoring, and prioritized action items.

### Engineering Decision Support

AI-generated delivery plans from a single feature request. The engine analyzes dependency graphs, historical velocity, team capacity, and incident patterns to produce complete engineering plans with effort estimation, resource allocation, risk mitigation, and sprint breakdown — reducing planning overhead from days to seconds.

## Why engineering intelligence is infrastructure

The engineering organization of 2026 runs 47+ services, deploys 800+ times per year, and generates more telemetry than any human team can process. The gap between monitoring and intelligence is the single largest source of operational waste in the enterprise.

Organizations that react to failures will always be outpaced by organizations that predict them. Teams that estimate with gut feel will always be outperformed by teams that generate plans from data. Leadership that reviews incident postmortems after the fact will always be outmaneuvered by leadership that receives intelligence before the incident occurs.

OrbitForesight is the infrastructure for that transformation. Every deployment trains the prediction model. Every incident improves the investigation engine. Every investigation enriches the knowledge graph. The platform learns continuously — becoming more accurate, more comprehensive, and more valuable with every cycle.

**The future of engineering operations is not better dashboards. It is decision intelligence that connects engineering data to executive action.**

## Setup

```bash
# Clone
git clone https://github.com/your-org/orbitforesight.git
cd orbitforesight

# Frontend
cd frontend
npm install
npm run dev
# → http://localhost:5173

# Backend (separate terminal)
cd backend
pip install -r requirements.txt
uvicorn main:app --reload --port 8000
```

The platform loads with full mock data and no external dependencies. Open `http://localhost:5173` to begin.

### Production build

```bash
cd frontend
npm run build
# Output: frontend/dist/ — code-split, optimized, zero warnings
```
